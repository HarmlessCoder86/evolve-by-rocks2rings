Vagus Nerve Reset

Your vagus nerve is the highway between your brain and body. These exercises tell your nervous system: "You're safe now."

üßä

Cold Water Reset

‚è± 30 seconds

Splash cold water on your face or hold ice cubes. The cold activates your dive reflex and instantly calms your heart rate.

Tap for step-by-step ‚Üí
  1. Get a bowl of cold water or grab ice cubes
  2. Take a deep breath in
  3. Splash cold water on your face, especially forehead and cheeks
  4. Hold for 15-30 seconds
  5. Notice your heart rate slowing down
  6. Repeat if needed ‚Äî your body is resetting

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

‚è± 2 minutes

Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. Brings you back to the present moment.

Tap for step-by-step ‚Üí
  1. 5 things you SEE: Look around slowly. Name them out loud if you can.
  2. 4 things you can TOUCH: Feel the texture of your clothes, the chair, your skin.
  3. 3 things you HEAR: Close your eyes. What sounds are there?
  4. 2 things you SMELL: Breathe deeply. Even "nothing" is an answer.
  5. 1 thing you TASTE: Run your tongue over your lips. Sip water.
  6. Take one more deep breath. You're here. You're present. You're okay.

Bee Breath (Humming)

‚è± 1 minute

The vibration of humming directly stimulates your vagus nerve. It's like giving your nervous system a massage from the inside.

Tap for step-by-step ‚Üí
  1. Sit comfortably. Close your eyes.
  2. Place your fingers gently over your ears (optional)
  3. Take a deep breath in through your nose
  4. As you exhale, make a long "hmmmmm" sound
  5. Feel the vibration in your chest, throat, and face
  6. Repeat 5-6 times. Notice the calm spreading through you.

Eye Movement Reset

‚è± 30 seconds

Slow lateral eye movements activate the parasympathetic nervous system. It's the same principle behind EMDR therapy.

Tap for step-by-step ‚Üí
  1. Keep your head still, facing forward
  2. Slowly look as far right as you can
  3. Hold for 15-30 seconds until you sigh or yawn
  4. Return to center
  5. Now look as far left as you can
  6. Hold until you feel a release (sigh, yawn, or swallow)

Self-Holding Hug

‚è± 1 minute

Cross your arms and hold your own shoulders. Gentle pressure releases oxytocin and tells your body it's being cared for.

Tap for step-by-step ‚Üí
  1. Cross your arms over your chest
  2. Place each hand on the opposite shoulder
  3. Squeeze gently ‚Äî like you're hugging yourself
  4. Slowly alternate tapping left, right, left, right
  5. Breathe slowly as you tap
  6. Whisper: "I'm here. I've got you. You're safe."

Long Exhale Sighing

‚è± 1 minute

A physiological sigh ‚Äî two quick inhales through the nose, one long exhale through the mouth ‚Äî is the fastest way to calm down.

Tap for step-by-step ‚Üí
  1. Inhale quickly through your nose
  2. Immediately inhale again (a second sniff) to fully expand your lungs
  3. Now exhale slowly through your mouth ‚Äî as long as possible
  4. Let the exhale be audible. Sigh it out.
  5. Repeat 3-5 times
  6. This is what your body naturally does when it's trying to calm itself
